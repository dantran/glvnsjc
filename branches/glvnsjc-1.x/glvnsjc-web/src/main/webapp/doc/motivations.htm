Motivations/Problems

 

For the passed five years teaching at GLNV St. Patrick, I have seen struggles and chaos when dealing with the management of student records at my school.  I am sure other schools also going through the same obstacles.  So here is a list of struggles we have been through each school year.

 

  1. Before school year starts, Frere sends out to each school a list of students in an Excel spreadsheet.  A principal of each school splits the spreadsheet into multiple worksheets with multiple classes.  Some classes are oversize and required to break into subclasses.  Working with multiple spreadsheets is not a pleasant task. 

 

  1. Frere then sends addition lists due to receiving new student applications.  Again we have to compare the new lists with the old one.  Hard work for those who are responsible for these tasks.

 

  1. School starts and students are moved to other classes due to many reasons. Most noticeable are Việt Ngữ classes.

 

  1. Teachers compose his/her own list and email to the Principal a new list that does not have crucial information like student id, middle name, etc. Principal merges the class lists with original one and send back to Frere.  Due to the complexity of the merge task, some schools just never finish it.

 

  1. Frere manually scan through the lists sending from principals and adjust his database.

 

  1. At the end of school year, teacher grades the student and sends the VN and GL grades in 2 separate lists to school principal who merges them again before sending to Frere.  The new list may never make it.

 

  1. Frere again manually scan through the lists and update his database.

 

  1. At the end of school year, each GL teacher coordinates with VN teachers regarding the grades.  This task takes lot of energy and time and we end up writing the student information manually.

 

  1. Award certificates also manually written.  I think this case the teacher can professionally print the certificates, but other tasks take much of energy and the best way to get thing done is to write it up.

 

  1. Don’t have a feeling of doing a good job at the end of school year.

 

In summary, the main problem we see here is that there are so many databases to manage: one from Frere, one for each school principal, and one for each GL and VN class.  Merging database with different format is an impossible task.

Solutions

 

GLVNSJC Database System (GLVNSJCDB) provides a WEB centric database system, which everyone can work on. Each school can manage its own students with Frere has access to all schools.

 

Roadmap

 

Starting school year 2003-2004, we will use GLVNSJCDB in parallel with the current Frere’s MS Access.  Data from Frere’s database can be exported to GLVNDBSJC.  This redundant system is to prevent the case where GLVNSJC is not working as desired.  The server is deployed at my house over 256K uplink.  The link can handle up to 15 users concurrently.

 

If everything goes well in school year 2003-2004, we can move to single database system and deploy GLVNDBSJC in a paid hosted system starting school year 2004-2005.  The estimate cost is $15/month.
